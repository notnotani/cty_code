Here are some audio samples showing the intricacies and nuances of tabla playing from all across India.


Here is a type of composition called a "peshkar" which is common in Northern India. It is a generally short piece which contains an intense buildup leading to the climax. This particular peshkar was performed but Ustad Natthu Khan.



This piece is called a "rela" which derives from the English word "rail". This implies that the piece is very fast and contains precise hand movements. This was performed by Bickram Ghosh at the popular Darbar festival in 2015.



This piece is interesting in its refusal to not follow a particular structure. It, however, shows off the various sounds of the tabla and how they can be manipulated to evoke certain emotions in people. This is also a recently performed piece, being performed by the great Shahbaz Hussain in 2015.



This gem from 1992 shows off the incredible harmony that can be achieved when a tabla is but together with a string instrument like the sarangi. The two players, Ustad Miyan Shaukat Hussain Khan Sahib on tabla and Ustad Sultan Khan on the sarangi take turns going back and forth, building up to the climx at the end.



This is a live performance by Zakir Hussain. It is best to let the music speak for itself here.
