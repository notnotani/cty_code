The Great Maestros of Tabla

Many influential tabla players have made their mark throughout the tabla's history. But who are they, and what did they contribute?

Zakir Hussain

Who He Is

Zakir Hussain was born on March 9th, 1951 in Mumbai. He learned tabla from his father, Alla Rakha. He is appreciated both in the field of percussion and in the music world at large as an international phenomenon. A classical tabla virtuoso of the highest order, his consistently brilliant and exciting performances have not only established him as a national treasure in India, but gained him worldwide fame. He has been the favorite accompanist to most of India’s greatest classical musicians and dancers. His playing is marked by uncanny intuition and masterful improvisational dexterity, founded in profound knowledge and study.

His Life

As a child prodigy, Zakir Hussain was touring by the age of 12. He first came to the United States in 1970. He performs all over the world throughout the year. He has composed and recorded innumerable albums and soundtracks, and has received widespread recognition as a composer for his many ensembles and collaborations. He has composed soundtracks for the films “In Custody,” Ismail Merchant’s directorial debut, “Little Buddha” by Bernardo Bertolucci, for which Zakir Hussain composed, performed and acted as Indian music advisor, and “Vanaprastham,” chosen to be screened at the Cannes Film Festival in May, 1999.

Ahmedjan Thirakawa

Who He Is

Ahmedjan Thirakawa was born in 1891 or 1892 in Muradabad, Uttar Pradesh. His father, Hussain Baksh Khan, was a famous Sarangi and tabla player, and his uncle, Sher Khan, was a reputed tabla player. On his mother’s side, his grandfather Kalander Baksh Khan and uncles Faiyaz Khan and Baksh Khan were also well known tabla players. This implies that he was born in an atmosphere of tabla. His father and uncle initiated him into tabla. Later he also received guidance from his two maternal uncles.
